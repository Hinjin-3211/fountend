# System Introduction

**System**
A system is a collection of interrelated components that function together to achieve some outcome.
系统是一组相互关联的部分组成的集合体，这些部分协同工作以达成某些结果。

**Natural System**
Natural System are the systems in the material world, including both non-living and living systems, as well as social and cognitive systems.
自然系统是物质世界中的系统，包括非生物系统、生物系统以及社会和认知系统。

**Artificial System**
Artificial systems exist within natural systems, and there is an interface between them that influences and permeates each other.
人造系统存在于自然系统之中，它们之间存在一个互相影响和渗透的接口。

**Composite System**
Composite systems include both artificial and natural systems.
复合系统包含人造系统和自然系统。

# Project Initiation

**Project**
A project is identified when someone in the organization identifies a business vision to build a system.
当组织中的某个人识别出构建系统的商业愿景时，一个项目就被确定了。

项目可能支持新的市场活动、接触新类型的客户或改善与供应商的互动。
Projects might support a new marketing campaign, reaching out to a new type of customer, or improving interactions with suppliers.

**Project Vision**
A specific, inspiring statement describing the project’s desired future state. Acts as a shared goal for the team to commit to.
描述项目期望未来状态的具体且鼓舞人心的陈述。作为团队共同追求的目标。

**Project Sponsor**
Recognizes the strong business need for a system and has an interest in seeing the system succeed.
认识到系统强烈的业务需求并有兴趣看到系统成功的人。

**Business Opportunity**
Results from market requirement changes, technological progress, policy changes, competitor missteps.
由市场需求变化、技术进步、政策变化、竞争对手失误等原因引发的业务机会。

**Business Goals**
Forward-looking: Future state. Incentive: Inspire team member. Valuable: Economic value and social value.
具有前瞻性：未来的状态；激励作用：激励团队成员；有价值：经济价值和社会价值。

**Business Values**
Financial Goals:

- Capture a market share of X% within Y months.
- Increase market share in country X by Y% in Z months.
- Reach a sales volume of X units or revenue of $Y within Z months.
- Achieve X% profit or return on investment.
- Achieve positive cash flow on this product within Y months.

财务目标：

- 在 Y 个月内占领 X%的市场份额。
- 在 Z 个月内将国家 X 的市场份额提高 Y%。
- 在 Z 个月内达到 X 个单位的销售量或$Y 的收入。
- 实现 X%的利润或投资回报率。
- 在 Y 个月内实现该产品的正现金流。

Nonfinancial Goals:

- Achieve a customer satisfaction measure of at least X within Y months of release.
- Increase transaction-processing productivity by X% and reduce data error rate to no more than Y%.

非财务目标：

- 在发布后的 Y 个月内达到至少 X 的客户满意度。
- 提高交易处理生产力 X%，并将数据错误率降低至不超过 Y%。

**Feasibility Analysis**
Feasibility study is a comprehensive evaluation of the application project, assessing all important factors that affect its success, in order to measure its likelihood of success.
可行性研究是对应用项目的全面评估，评估所有影响其成功的因素，以衡量其成功的可能性。

**Technical Feasibility**
To assess the technical feasibility of the project, the extent to which the system can be successfully designed, developed, and installed by the IT group.
评估项目的技术可行性，即 IT 小组能否成功设计、开发和安装该系统。

**Economic Feasibility**
Identifies the financial risk associated with the project. It attempts to answer this question: Should we build the system?
识别与项目相关的财务风险。它试图回答这个问题：我们应该建立这个系统吗？

**Legal Feasibility**
This assessment investigates whether any aspect of the proposed project conflicts with legal requirements.
此评估调查提议项目的任何方面是否与法律要求相冲突。

**Operational Feasibility**
Analyze and determine whether and how to meet the organization's needs by completing the project.
分析并确定通过完成项目来满足组织需求的可能性和方式。

**Social Feasibility**
Focuses on the acceptance and impact of the project in the social environment.
关注项目在社会环境中的接受度和影响。

# Business Requirement

**Introduction to Requirement**
Business Requirements are high-level goals that define the vision and scope of the project.
业务需求是定义项目愿景和范围的高层次目标。

**Requirement Definition (IEEE)**
A condition or capability needed by a user to solve a problem or achieve an objective.
用户为解决问题或达成目标所需的条件或能力。

A condition or capability that must be met or possessed by a system or system component to satisfy a contract, standard, specification, or other formally imposed document.
系统或系统组件必须满足或具备的条件或能力，以符合合同、标准、规范或其他正式规定的文档。

A documented representation of a condition or capability as in 1 or 2.
如上述 1 或 2 所述条件或能力的文档化表示。

Requirements are usually used to express "what to do" rather than "how to do".
需求通常用于表达“做什么”而不是“怎么做”。

**Functions of Requirement**
Fully understand the real business problems, and as the basis of software design;
充分理解真实的业务问题，并作为软件设计的基础；

Provide accurate basis for cost, time and risk estimation of software projects;
为软件项目的成本、时间和风险估算提供准确依据；

Reduce development workload and avoid wasting time.
减少开发工作量并避免浪费时间。

Baselines of evolution and change; Part of a formal contract; Criteria and basis for final acceptance test.
演变和变更的基线；正式合同的一部分；最终验收测试的标准和基础。

**Different Levels of Requirements**
Business Requirement: High-level goal requirements for the system define the vision and scope of the project.
业务需求：系统的高层次目标需求定义了项目的愿景和范围。

User Requirement: What kind of functions should the system provide for users?
用户需求：系统应为用户提供什么样的功能？

Functional Requirement: Detailed descriptions of specific functionalities the system needs to implement.
功能需求：系统需要实现的具体功能的详细描述。

System Constraint Requirement: Limitations on the system's operation or implementation.
系统约束需求：对系统操作或实现的限制。

External Interfaces: Descriptions of how the system interacts with external systems.
外部接口：系统如何与外部系统交互的描述。

Quality Attributes: Non-functional requirements such as performance, reliability, and usability.
质量属性：性能、可靠性、可用性等非功能性需求。

Business Rules: Specific rules that govern the operations and decisions within the business processes.
业务规则：治理业务流程中的操作和决策的具体规则。

# Use Case Diagram

**Use Case**
A use case is a list of actions or event steps typically defining the interactions between a role and a system to achieve a goal.
用例是通常定义角色与系统之间交互以实现目标的动作或事件步骤列表。

The role can be a human, another system, or time-based events.
角色可以是人类、另一个系统或基于时间的事件。

**Actors**
An actor specifies a role played by a user or any other system that interacts with the subject.
参与者指定了用户或与主题交互的任何其他系统所扮演的角色。

Primary Actor: The primary actor of a use case is the stakeholder that requires assistance from the system to perform an action.
主要参与者：用例的主要参与者是需要系统帮助执行操作的利益相关者。

Supporting Actor: A supporting actor is one that provides a service needed by the system under design.
辅助参与者：辅助参与者是提供设计中的系统所需服务的参与者。

**System Boundary**
The system boundary defines the scope of what is being modeled.
系统边界定义了建模范围。

Everything inside the box represents elements within the system; everything outside the box represents elements external to the system.
框内的所有内容代表系统内部的元素；框外的所有内容代表系统外部的元素。

**Relationships in Use Case Diagram**
Association: A communication path between actors and use cases. It indicates that the actor participates in the use case.
关联：参与者和用例之间的通信路径。它表示参与者参与了该用例。

Include: One use case includes the behavior of another use case. This allows common behaviors to be reused across multiple use cases.
包含：一个用例包含另一个用例的行为。这允许在多个用例中重用公共行为。

Extend: One use case extends the behavior of another use case, adding optional steps under certain conditions.
扩展：一个用例扩展了另一个用例的行为，在某些条件下添加可选步骤。

Generalization: A generalization relationship shows that one use case or actor is a specialization of another.
泛化：泛化关系表明一个用例或参与者是另一个的特化。

**Use Case Description**
A detailed textual description of each use case which includes:
每个用例的详细文本描述，包括：

- Precondition: Conditions that must be true before the use case starts.
  前置条件：用例开始前必须为真的条件。
- Postcondition: Conditions that will be true after the use case completes successfully.
  后置条件：用例成功完成后将为真的条件。
- Main Success Scenario: A sequence of steps describing the normal flow of interaction.
  主要成功场景：描述正常交互流程的一系列步骤。
- Extensions: Variations from the main success scenario based on different conditions.
  扩展：基于不同条件的主要成功场景的变化。

# Behavioral Diagrams

## Behavioral Diagram Overview

**Behavioral Diagram**
Behavioral diagrams depict the dynamic aspects of a system, focusing on how different parts of the system operate and interact with each other over time.
行为图描绘系统的动态方面，关注系统不同部分如何随着时间进行操作及相互作用。

**Types of Behavioral Diagrams**

- Use Case Diagram: Shows interactions between actors and use cases within a system.
- Sequence Diagram: Depicts how objects interact in a particular scenario of a use case.
- Activity Diagram: Represents the flow from one activity to another within a system.
- State Machine Diagram: Describes the states an object can be in and transitions between those states.

用例图：显示系统内参与者与用例之间的交互。
序列图：描述在用例特定场景中对象如何交互。
活动图：表示系统内从一个活动到另一个活动的流动。
状态机图：描述对象可以处于的状态及其间的转换。

## Use Case Diagram

**Use Case Diagram Components**

- Actors: Entities that interact with the system or business processes.
- Use Cases: Represent specific functionalities or services provided by the system.
- Relationships: Associations, include, extend, and generalization relationships between actors and use cases.

参与者：与系统或业务流程交互的实体。
用例：代表系统提供的具体功能或服务。
关系：参与者和用例之间的关联、包含、扩展和泛化关系。

## Sequence Diagram

**Sequence Diagram Components**

- Objects/Lifelines: Vertical lines representing instances of classes participating in the interaction.
- Messages: Horizontal arrows showing communication between objects.
- Activation Bars: Rectangular bars indicating when an object is active during the interaction.

对象/生命线：代表参与交互的类实例的垂直线。
消息：水平箭头，显示对象之间的通信。
激活条：矩形条，指示对象在交互期间何时处于活跃状态。

## Activity Diagram

**Activity Diagram Components**

- Activities: Rounded rectangles representing actions or steps within a process.
- Control Flow: Arrows showing the order in which activities are executed.
- Decision Points: Diamonds indicating choices based on conditions.
- Swimlanes: Divisions showing responsibilities for activities among different actors or departments.

活动：圆角矩形，代表过程中的动作或步骤。
控制流：箭头，显示活动执行的顺序。
决策点：菱形，指示基于条件的选择。
泳道：划分不同参与者或部门之间活动的责任。

## State Machine Diagram

**State Machine Diagram Components**

- States: Rounded rectangles representing possible conditions of an object.
- Transitions: Arrows showing changes from one state to another triggered by events.
- Initial State: A filled black circle indicating the start point.
- Final State: A filled black circle with a border indicating the end point.

状态：圆角矩形，代表对象可能的状态。
转换：箭头，显示由事件触发的状态变化。
初始状态：填充的黑色圆圈，表示起点。
最终状态：带有边框的填充黑色圆圈，表示终点。

## Key Concepts

**Dynamic Aspects**
Focus on the behavior of systems over time, emphasizing interactions and flows rather than static structures.
关注系统随时间的行为，强调交互和流而不是静态结构。

**Interaction**
The way objects communicate and work together to achieve common goals within a system.
对象在系统内通过交流和协作实现共同目标的方式。

**Flow**
The sequence of steps or stages through which a process progresses from start to finish.
过程从开始到结束所经历的一系列步骤或阶段。

# Object-Oriented Technology

## Introduction to Object-Oriented Technology

**Object-Oriented Technology**
Object-oriented technology is a method of development that uses "objects" to design applications and computer programs.
面向对象技术是一种使用“对象”来设计应用程序和计算机程序的开发方法。

It focuses on modeling real-world entities as objects which contain both data and methods for manipulating that data.
它侧重于将现实世界的实体建模为包含数据及其操作方法的对象。

**Key Concepts in Object-Oriented Technology**

- **Encapsulation**: Binding the data and methods that operate on the data within a single unit, i.e., an object.
- **Abstraction**: Hiding complex realities while exposing only the necessary parts.
- **Inheritance**: Allowing new classes to inherit properties and behaviors from existing classes.
- **Polymorphism**: Ability to process objects differently based on their data type or class.

封装：将数据及其操作方法绑定在一个单元内，即一个对象。
抽象：隐藏复杂的现实，仅暴露必要的部分。
继承：允许新类从现有类继承属性和行为。
多态性：根据对象的数据类型或类以不同方式处理对象的能力。

## Classes and Objects

**Class**
A class is a blueprint for creating objects. It defines a set of properties (attributes) and methods (functions) that the created objects will have.
类是创建对象的蓝图。它定义了一组属性（变量）和方法（函数），这些属性和方法将在创建的对象中体现。

**Object**
An object is an instance of a class. It has its own state, behavior, and identity.
对象是类的一个实例。它有自己的状态、行为和标识。

## Inheritance

**Inheritance**
Inheritance enables one class to acquire the properties and functionalities of another class.
继承使得一个类能够获得另一个类的属性和功能。

**Types of Inheritance**

- Single inheritance: A subclass inherits from a single superclass.
- Multiple inheritance: A subclass inherits from more than one superclass.
- Multilevel inheritance: A subclass inherits from a class that is itself a subclass of another class.
- Hierarchical inheritance: Multiple subclasses inherit from a single superclass.
- Hybrid inheritance: Combination of more than one type of inheritance.

单一继承：子类继承自单个父类。
多重继承：子类继承自多个父类。
多级继承：子类继承自一个本身就是另一类的子类的类。
层次继承：多个子类继承自一个父类。
混合继承：多种继承类型的组合。

## Polymorphism

**Polymorphism**
Polymorphism allows objects of different classes to be treated as objects of a common superclass.
多态性允许不同类的对象被当作一个共同超类的对象对待。

**Types of Polymorphism**

- Compile-time polymorphism (method overloading): Methods with the same name but different parameters.
- Runtime polymorphism (method overriding): Methods in a subclass that override methods in its superclass.

编译时多态（方法重载）：名称相同但参数不同的方法。
运行时多态（方法覆盖）：子类中的方法覆盖其父类中的方法。

## Encapsulation

**Encapsulation**
Encapsulation hides the internal state of an object and requires all interaction to be performed through well-defined interfaces.
封装隐藏了对象的内部状态，并要求所有交互都通过明确定义的接口进行。

**Benefits of Encapsulation**

- Increases reusability and maintainability of code.
- Reduces complexity by providing clear boundaries between components.
- Enhances security by hiding the internal workings of an object.

增加了代码的可重用性和可维护性。
通过提供组件之间的清晰边界减少复杂性。
通过隐藏对象的内部工作原理增强安全性。

## Abstraction

**Abstraction**
Abstraction simplifies complex systems by modeling classes appropriate to the problem domain.
抽象通过为问题域建模适当的类来简化复杂的系统。

**Abstract Class**
An abstract class cannot be instantiated and may contain abstract methods without implementations.
抽象类不能被实例化，可以包含没有实现的抽象方法。

**Interface**
An interface defines a contract for what a class can do, without specifying how it does it.
接口定义了一个类能做什么的契约，而不指定它是如何做的。

# Business Object Model

## Introduction to Business Object Model

**Business Object Model**
A business object model represents the structure of the data and information used by business processes.
业务对象模型表示业务流程使用的数据和信息的结构。

It includes entities, attributes, relationships, and constraints that define how data is organized and manipulated within an organization.
它包括定义数据在组织内如何组织和操作的实体、属性、关系和约束。

## Key Components of Business Object Model

### Entities

**Entity**
An entity is a distinct object or concept within the business domain that needs to be represented in the system.
实体是业务领域中需要在系统中表示的独特对象或概念。

For example, Customer, Order, Product are all examples of entities in a typical business context.
例如，在典型的业务环境中，客户、订单、产品都是实体的例子。

### Attributes

**Attribute**
Attributes describe the characteristics or properties of an entity.
属性描述实体的特征或属性。

Each entity has one or more attributes that provide specific details about the entity.
每个实体有一个或多个提供实体具体细节的属性。

For instance, a Customer entity might have attributes like Name, Address, and Email.
例如，客户实体可能有姓名、地址和电子邮件等属性。

### Relationships

**Relationship**
Relationships define how different entities are connected or related to each other.
关系定义不同的实体如何相互连接或关联。

Common types of relationships include:
常见的关系类型包括：

- One-to-One (1:1)
- One-to-Many (1:N)
- Many-to-Many (M:N)

一对一（1:1）
一对多（1:N）
多对多（M:N）

### Constraints

**Constraint**
Constraints are rules that govern the data stored in the business object model.
约束是管理业务对象模型中存储的数据的规则。

Examples of constraints include:
约束的例子包括：

- Primary key constraint: Ensures that each record in a table is unique.
- Foreign key constraint: Ensures referential integrity between tables.
- Check constraint: Ensures that values meet certain criteria.

主键约束：确保表中的每条记录都是唯一的。
外键约束：确保表之间的引用完整性。
检查约束：确保值符合某些标准。

## Modeling Techniques

### Entity-Relationship Diagram (ERD)

**Entity-Relationship Diagram (ERD)**
An ERD visually represents the entities, their attributes, and the relationships between them.
ERD 直观地表示实体、它们的属性以及它们之间的关系。

It helps in understanding the structure of the data and how it is interconnected.
它有助于理解数据的结构及其相互连接的方式。

### Class Diagram

**Class Diagram**
A class diagram is a type of static structure diagram that describes the structure of a system by showing its classes, attributes, methods, and the relationships among objects.
类图是一种静态结构图，通过显示系统的类、属性、方法及对象间的关系来描述系统的结构。

It provides a comprehensive overview of the system's architecture and design.
它提供了系统架构和设计的全面概述。

### Object Diagram

**Object Diagram**
An object diagram shows a snapshot of instances of classes at a particular point in time.
对象图显示了特定时间点上类实例的快照。

It is useful for verifying the correctness of class diagrams by showing concrete examples of objects and their interactions.
通过显示对象及其交互的具体示例，它对于验证类图的正确性非常有用。

## Benefits of Business Object Model

**Improved Understanding**
The business object model improves understanding of the business domain by clearly defining entities, attributes, and relationships.

# User Requirement

## Introduction to User Requirements

**User Requirement**
User requirements are descriptions of what the system should do from the perspective of the end user. They focus on the functional and non-functional needs of the users.
用户需求是从最终用户的角度对系统应做什么的描述。它们侧重于用户的功能性和非功能性需求。

These requirements ensure that the system meets the expectations and needs of its users, making it both useful and usable.
这些需求确保系统满足用户的期望和需要，使其既实用又可用。

## Key Concepts in User Requirements

### Functional Requirements

**Functional Requirements**
Functional requirements describe specific behaviors or functions that the system must perform to be considered complete and operational.
功能需求描述了系统必须执行的具体行为或功能，以便被认为是完整且可操作的。

For example, a login function, data entry forms, report generation, etc., are examples of functional requirements.
例如，登录功能、数据输入表单、报告生成等都是功能需求的例子。

### Non-Functional Requirements

**Non-Functional Requirements**
Non-functional requirements specify criteria that judge the operation of a system, rather than specific behaviors.
非功能性需求指定了评估系统运行的标准，而不是具体的行为。

Examples include performance requirements (response time), usability requirements, security requirements, and reliability requirements.
例如包括性能要求（响应时间）、可用性要求、安全要求和可靠性要求。

### Use Cases

**Use Case**
A use case is a detailed description of how users will interact with the system to achieve a specific goal.
用例是用户如何与系统交互以实现特定目标的详细描述。

It includes the sequence of actions and the various possible outcomes.
它包括一系列动作和各种可能的结果。

### User Stories

**User Story**
A user story is a simple, concise description of a feature told from the perspective of the person who desires the new capability, usually a user or customer of the system.
用户故事是从希望新功能的人的角度讲述的功能的简单、简洁的描述，通常是系统的用户或客户。

Example: As a [type of user], I want [some goal] so that [some reason].
示例：作为[用户类型]，我希望[某个目标]，以便[某个原因]。

### Prototypes

**Prototype**
A prototype is a preliminary model of something, used to evaluate the feasibility or usability of a design before full development begins.
原型是某事物的初步模型，在全面开发开始前用于评估设计的可行性和可用性。

Prototypes can be paper-based sketches or interactive digital models.
原型可以是基于纸张的草图或交互式的数字模型。

## Gathering User Requirements

### Interviews

**Interviews**
Interviews involve direct communication with stakeholders to gather detailed information about their needs and expectations.
访谈涉及与利益相关者的直接沟通，以收集有关其需求和期望的详细信息。

Structured interviews have predefined questions, while unstructured interviews allow for more open-ended discussion.
结构化访谈有预定义的问题，而非结构化访谈允许更开放的讨论。

### Surveys/Questionnaires

**Surveys/Questionnaires**
Surveys or questionnaires are tools used to collect quantitative data from a large number of respondents.
调查或问卷是用于从大量受访者那里收集定量数据的工具。

They can provide insights into user preferences, satisfaction levels, and pain points.
它们可以提供关于用户偏好、满意度水平和痛点的见解。

### Workshops

**Workshops**
Workshops bring together stakeholders in a collaborative environment to brainstorm ideas and solutions.
研讨会将利益相关者聚集在一个协作环境中，共同集思广益并提出解决方案。

They encourage active participation and help build consensus around requirements.
它们鼓励积极参与，并有助于围绕需求达成共识。

### Observation

**Observation**
Observation involves watching users as they perform tasks to understand their workflows and identify areas for improvement.
观察包括观看用户执行任务的过程，以了解其工作流程并确定改进领域。

This method provides valuable insights into real-world usage scenarios.
这种方法为实际使用场景提供了宝贵的见解。

## Documenting User Requirements

### Requirement Specification Document

**Requirement Specification Document**
The requirement specification document is a formal document that outlines all the functional and non-functional requirements of the system.
需求规格说明书是一份正式文档，概述了系统的所有功能性和非功能性需求。

It serves as a reference point throughout the project lifecycle.
在整个项目生命周期中，它作为参考点。

### Use Case Descriptions

**Use Case Descriptions**
Use case descriptions detail each use case, including preconditions, postconditions, main success scenario, and alternative paths.
用例描述详细说明每个用例，包括前置条件、后置条件、主要成功场景和替代路径。

They help ensure that all necessary interactions are covered and understood.
它们有助于确保所有必要的交互都被涵盖和理解。

### User Stories

**User Stories**
User stories capture requirements in a simple, understandable format that focuses on the user's perspective.
用户故事以简单易懂的格式捕捉需求，重点在于用户视角。

They help prioritize features and ensure alignment with user needs.
它们有助于优先考虑功能，并确保与用户需求保持一致。

# System Use Case Model

## Introduction to System Use Case Model

**System Use Case Model**
A system use case model describes how users interact with the system and how the system behaves in response to those interactions.
系统用例模型描述了用户如何与系统交互以及系统如何响应这些交互的行为。

It focuses on functional requirements and provides a clear picture of what the system is supposed to do from the user's perspective.
它侧重于功能需求，并从用户的角度清晰地展示了系统应该做什么。

## Key Components of System Use Case Model

### Actors

**Actor**
An actor represents a role played by a user or another system that interacts with the system being modeled.
参与者代表用户或另一个与被建模系统交互的系统所扮演的角色。

Actors can be human users, external systems, or even time-based events.
参与者可以是人类用户、外部系统，甚至是基于时间的事件。

### Use Cases

**Use Case**
A use case describes a sequence of actions that the system performs to yield an observable result of value to a particular actor.
用例描述了系统执行的一系列动作，以产生对特定参与者有价值的可观察结果。

Each use case represents a specific functionality provided by the system.
每个用例代表系统提供的具体功能。

### Relationships Between Actors and Use Cases

#### Association

**Association**
An association indicates that an actor participates in a use case.
关联表示参与者参与了一个用例。

This relationship is shown as a solid line between the actor and the use case.
这种关系显示为参与者和用例之间的一条实线。

#### Include

**Include**
An include relationship indicates that one use case includes the behavior of another use case.
包含关系表示一个用例包含了另一个用例的行为。

This allows for reuse of common behaviors across multiple use cases.
这允许在多个用例中重用常见的行为。

#### Extend

**Extend**
An extend relationship indicates that one use case extends the behavior of another use case under certain conditions.
扩展关系表示一个用例在某些条件下扩展了另一个用例的行为。

This adds optional steps to the base use case based on specific conditions.
这基于特定条件向基础用例添加了可选步骤。

#### Generalization

**Generalization**
A generalization relationship indicates that one actor or use case is a specialization of another.
泛化关系表示一个参与者或用例是另一个的特化。

This allows for inheritance of properties and behaviors.
这允许继承属性和行为。

## Steps to Create a System Use Case Model

### Identify Actors

**Identify Actors**
Identify all potential actors who will interact with the system.
识别所有可能与系统交互的参与者。

Consider both human users and external systems.
考虑人类用户和外部系统。

### Identify Use Cases

**Identify Use Cases**
For each actor, identify the use cases that describe the interactions they have with the system.
对于每个参与者，识别描述他们与系统交互的用例。

Focus on the functionalities that are important to the actor.
关注对参与者重要的功能。

### Define Relationships

**Define Relationships**
Define the relationships between actors and use cases.
定义参与者和用例之间的关系。

Use associations, includes, extends, and generalizations to show these relationships.
使用关联、包含、扩展和泛化来展示这些关系。

### Document Details

**Document Details**
Document detailed descriptions of each use case, including:
记录每个用例的详细描述，包括：

- **Preconditions**: Conditions that must be true before the use case starts.
  前置条件：用例开始前必须为真的条件。
- **Postconditions**: Conditions that will be true after the use case completes successfully.
  后置条件：用例成功完成后将为真的条件。
- **Main Success Scenario**: A sequence of steps describing the normal flow of interaction.
  主要成功场景：描述正常交互流程的一系列步骤。
- **Extensions**: Variations from the main success scenario based on different conditions.
  扩展：基于不同条件的主要成功场景的变化。

## Benefits of System Use Case Model

### Improved Communication

**Improved Communication**
The system use case model serves as a communication tool between stakeholders, developers, and business analysts.
系统用例模型作为利益相关者、开发人员和业务分析师之间的沟通工具。

It ensures everyone has a shared understanding of the system's requirements.
它确保每个人都对系统的需求有共同的理解。

### Clear Functional Requirements

**Clear Functional Requirements**
By focusing on specific functionalities and their interactions, the model helps clarify what the system needs to do.
通过专注于具体功能及其交互，该模型有助于澄清系统需要做什么。

### Reusability

**Reusability**
Common behaviors captured through include and extend relationships can be reused across multiple use cases.
通过包含和扩展关系捕获的常见行为可以在多个用例中重用。

### Prioritization

**Prioritization**
Use cases help prioritize development efforts by highlighting the most critical functionalities.
用例通过突出最关键的功用来帮助优先安排开发工作。

# System Object Model

## Introduction to System Object Model

**System Object Model**
A system object model represents the structure of the data and information used by the system, including entities, attributes, relationships, and constraints.
系统对象模型表示系统使用的数据和信息的结构，包括实体、属性、关系和约束。

It provides a blueprint for how these components interact within the system and is crucial for defining both the static structure and dynamic behavior of the software.
它提供了这些组件如何在系统内交互的蓝图，对于定义软件的静态结构和动态行为至关重要。

## Key Components of System Object Model

### Entities

**Entity**
An entity is a distinct object or concept within the domain that needs to be represented in the system.
实体是领域中需要在系统中表示的独特对象或概念。

For example, Customer, Order, and Product are all examples of entities in a typical business context.
例如，在典型的业务环境中，客户、订单和产品都是实体的例子。

### Attributes

**Attribute**
Attributes describe the characteristics or properties of an entity.
属性描述实体的特征或属性。

Each entity has one or more attributes that provide specific details about the entity.
每个实体有一个或多个提供实体具体细节的属性。

For instance, a Customer entity might have attributes like Name, Address, and Email.
例如，客户实体可能有姓名、地址和电子邮件等属性。

### Relationships

**Relationship**
Relationships define how different entities are connected or related to each other.
关系定义不同的实体如何相互连接或关联。

Common types of relationships include:
常见的关系类型包括：

- One-to-One (1:1)
- One-to-Many (1:N)
- Many-to-Many (M:N)

一对一（1:1）
一对多（1:N）
多对多（M:N）

### Constraints

**Constraint**
Constraints are rules that govern the data stored in the system object model.
约束是管理系统对象模型中存储的数据的规则。

Examples of constraints include:
约束的例子包括：

- Primary key constraint: Ensures that each record in a table is unique.
- Foreign key constraint: Ensures referential integrity between tables.
- Check constraint: Ensures that values meet certain criteria.

主键约束：确保表中的每条记录都是唯一的。
外键约束：确保表之间的引用完整性。
检查约束：确保值符合某些标准。

## Modeling Techniques

### Entity-Relationship Diagram (ERD)

**Entity-Relationship Diagram (ERD)**
An ERD visually represents the entities, their attributes, and the relationships between them.
ERD 直观地表示实体、它们的属性以及它们之间的关系。

It helps in understanding the structure of the data and how it is interconnected.
它有助于理解数据的结构及其相互连接的方式。

### Class Diagram

**Class Diagram**
A class diagram is a type of static structure diagram that describes the structure of a system by showing its classes, attributes, methods, and the relationships among objects.
类图是一种静态结构图，通过显示系统的类、属性、方法及对象间的关系来描述系统的结构。

It provides a comprehensive overview of the system's architecture and design.
它提供了系统架构和设计的全面概述。

### Object Diagram

**Object Diagram**
An object diagram shows a snapshot of instances of classes at a particular point in time.
对象图显示了特定时间点上类实例的快照。

It is useful for verifying the correctness of class diagrams by showing concrete examples of objects and their interactions.
通过显示对象及其交互的具体示例，它对于验证类图的正确性非常有用。

**Improved Understanding**

系统对象模型通过明确定义实体、属性和关系来提高对系统领域的理解。

**Facilitates Design and Development**

通过提供清晰的数据结构及其关系，系统对象模型促进了设计和开发过程。

### Enhances Data Integrity

**Enhances Data Integrity**
Constraints and relationships ensure data integrity and consistency throughout the system.
约束和关系确保系统中的数据完整性和一致性。

### Supports Evolution

**Supports Evolution**
The model supports the evolution of the system over time by allowing easy updates and modifications.
该模型通过允许轻松更新和修改支持系统随时间的演变。

**Guides Implementation**

系统对象模型通过提供必要组件及其交互的详细蓝图指导系统的实现。

# System Process Model

## Introduction to System Process Model

**System Process Model**
A system process model describes the dynamic behavior of a system, focusing on how processes and activities are executed over time.
系统过程模型描述了系统的动态行为，重点关注过程和活动如何随时间执行。

It helps in understanding workflows, interactions between components, and the sequence of operations within the system.
它有助于理解工作流、组件之间的交互以及系统内的操作顺序。

## Key Components of System Process Model

### Activities

**Activity**
An activity represents a unit of work or action that needs to be performed as part of a process.
活动代表作为过程的一部分需要执行的工作单元或动作。

Activities can be simple tasks or complex operations involving multiple steps.
活动可以是简单的任务，也可以是涉及多个步骤的复杂操作。

### Control Flow

**Control Flow**
Control flow defines the order in which activities are executed within the system.
控制流定义了系统内活动执行的顺序。

It includes decision points, loops, and parallel paths to manage different scenarios and conditions.
它包括决策点、循环和并行路径，以管理不同的场景和条件。

### Swimlanes

**Swimlane**
Swimlanes are used to organize activities by assigning them to specific actors or departments responsible for their execution.
泳道用于通过将活动分配给负责其执行的具体参与者或部门来组织活动。

This helps clarify responsibilities and roles within the system.
这有助于明确系统内的职责和角色。

### Events

**Event**
Events represent significant occurrences or changes in state that trigger transitions between activities.
事件代表重要的发生或状态变化，触发活动之间的转换。

Examples include start events, end events, and intermediate events.
示例包括开始事件、结束事件和中间事件。

### Gateways

**Gateway**
Gateways control the divergence and convergence of control flows, enabling decisions and branching based on certain conditions.
网关控制控制流的分叉和合并，允许根据某些条件进行决策和分支。

Types of gateways include exclusive, inclusive, and parallel gateways.
网关类型包括排他性、包容性和并行网关。

## Steps to Create a System Process Model

### Identify Activities

**Identify Activities**
Identify all activities involved in the process, breaking down larger tasks into smaller, manageable units.
识别过程中涉及的所有活动，将较大的任务分解为较小的、可管理的单元。

Consider both human actions and automated processes.
考虑人类行为和自动化过程。

### Define Control Flow

**Define Control Flow**
Determine the sequence in which activities should occur, including any conditional logic or loops.
确定活动应发生的顺序，包括任何条件逻辑或循环。

Use decision points and gateways to manage different paths through the process.
使用决策点和网关管理过程中的不同路径。

### Assign Responsibilities

**Assign Responsibilities**
Organize activities into swimlanes to assign responsibilities to specific actors or departments.
将活动组织成泳道，以将责任分配给特定的参与者或部门。

Ensure clarity on who is responsible for each task within the process.
确保对过程中的每个任务由谁负责有清晰的认识。

### Incorporate Events

**Incorporate Events**
Include relevant events that trigger transitions between activities or signify important milestones in the process.
包含相关事件，这些事件触发活动之间的转换或标志着过程中的重要里程碑。

Events help synchronize activities and ensure timely responses to changes.
事件有助于同步活动，并确保及时响应变化。

### Validate and Refine

**Validate and Refine**
Review the process model with stakeholders to validate its accuracy and completeness.
与利益相关者一起审查过程模型，验证其准确性和完整性。

Refine the model based on feedback and additional insights.
根据反馈和额外见解完善模型。

## Benefits of System Process Model

### Improved Efficiency

**Improved Efficiency**
The system process model helps identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies, leading to more streamlined workflows.
系统过程模型有助于识别瓶颈和低效之处，从而实现更流畅的工作流。

### Clear Communication

**Clear Communication**
It serves as a communication tool between stakeholders, developers, and business analysts, ensuring everyone has a shared understanding of the process.
它作为利益相关者、开发人员和业务分析师之间的沟通工具，确保每个人都对过程有共同的理解。

### Better Coordination

**Better Coordination**
By clearly defining roles and responsibilities, the model facilitates better coordination among team members and departments.
通过明确定义角色和职责，该模型促进了团队成员和部门之间的更好协调。

### Supports Continuous Improvement

**Supports Continuous Improvement**
The model supports continuous improvement efforts by providing a basis for analyzing and optimizing processes.
该模型通过提供分析和优化过程的基础支持持续改进努力。

### Facilitates Automation

**Facilitates Automation**
It provides a blueprint for automating repetitive tasks and integrating systems, enhancing overall productivity.
它为自动化重复任务和集成系统提供了蓝图，提高了整体生产力。

### Enhances Accountability

**Enhances Accountability**
By assigning responsibilities to specific actors, the model enhances accountability and ensures that tasks are completed as required.
通过将责任分配给具体的参与者，该模型增强了责任感，确保任务按要求完成。

# Writing Documents

## Introduction to Document Writing

**Document Writing**
Document writing is an integral part of the software development process. It involves creating documents that cover various aspects of a project, from initial requirements gathering through design, implementation, testing, and maintenance.
文档写作是软件开发过程中的一个重要组成部分。它包括创建涵盖项目各个方面（从初始需求收集到设计、实施、测试和维护）的文档。

Effective documentation helps ensure consistency, improves communication among team members, and serves as a valuable resource for future reference.
有效的文档有助于确保一致性，改善团队成员之间的沟通，并为未来参考提供宝贵的资源。

## Types of Documents

### Requirements Specification

**Requirements Specification**
This document details all functional and non-functional requirements of the system. It describes what the system should do and how it should behave under various conditions.
该文档详细说明了系统的所有功能性和非功能性需求。它描述了系统应该做什么以及在各种条件下的行为方式。

### Design Documents

**Design Documents**
These documents describe the architecture and detailed design of the system. They include diagrams, models, and descriptions of the system's components and their interactions.
这些文档描述了系统的架构和详细设计。它们包括系统组件及其交互的图示、模型和描述。

### User Manuals

**User Manuals**
User manuals provide instructions on how to use the system effectively. They are targeted at end users and should be clear, concise, and easy to follow.
用户手册提供了关于如何有效使用系统的说明。它们面向最终用户，应清晰、简洁且易于遵循。

### Test Plans and Reports

**Test Plans and Reports**
Test plans outline the strategy, scope, resources, and schedule for testing activities. Test reports summarize the results of these tests, including any issues found and their resolutions.
测试计划概述了测试活动的策略、范围、资源和时间表。测试报告总结了这些测试的结果，包括发现的问题及其解决方案。

## Steps in Writing Documents

### Planning

**Planning**
Identify the purpose of the document, its intended audience, and the key information that needs to be conveyed.
确定文档的目的、预期受众及需要传达的关键信息。

### Gathering Information

**Gathering Information**
Collect all necessary information from stakeholders, existing documents, and other sources.
从利益相关者、现有文档及其他来源收集所有必要的信息。

### Drafting

**Drafting**
Write the first draft of the document, focusing on organizing the information logically and clearly.
撰写文档的第一稿，重点在于逻辑清晰地组织信息。

### Reviewing

**Reviewing**
Have the document reviewed by peers or stakeholders to ensure accuracy, completeness, and clarity.
让同行或利益相关者审查文档，以确保其准确性、完整性和清晰度。

### Revising

**Revising**
Based on feedback received during the review phase, make necessary revisions to improve the document.
根据审阅阶段收到的反馈进行必要的修订，以改进文档。

### Publishing

**Publishing**
Finalize the document and distribute it to the intended audience. Ensure it is easily accessible and stored securely.
完成文档并分发给目标受众。确保其易于访问且安全存储。

## Best Practices for Writing Documents

### Be Clear and Concise

**Be Clear and Concise**
Use simple language and avoid jargon. Aim for clarity and brevity in your writing.
使用简单的语言并避免术语。力求写作的清晰和简洁。

### Use Visuals

**Use Visuals**
Incorporate diagrams, charts, and other visual aids to enhance understanding and engagement.
结合图表和其他视觉辅助工具以增强理解力和参与度。

### Keep Updated

**Keep Updated**
Regularly update documents to reflect changes in the project or system. Outdated documentation can lead to confusion and errors.
定期更新文档以反映项目或系统的变化。过时的文档可能导致混淆和错误。

### Get Feedback

**Get Feedback**
Involve stakeholders in reviewing your documents and encourage them to provide feedback. This ensures that the documents meet the needs of their audience.
让利益相关者参与审核你的文档，并鼓励他们提供反馈。这确保文档满足其受众的需求。

### Follow Standards

**Follow Standards**
Adhere to industry standards and best practices when writing documents. Consistency in format and style makes documents easier to read and understand.
编写文档时遵守行业标准和最佳实践。格式和风格的一致性使文档更易于阅读和理解。
